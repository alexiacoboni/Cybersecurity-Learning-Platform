Why the Attack Succeeded or Failed

Why the attack succeeded

  • Unlimited login attempts
  • No account lockout
  • No delay between attempts
  • Weak password

Why the attack failed

  • Rate limiting
  • Temporary lock
  • Password hashing
  • Login monitoring

Defense

Limit attempts per account and IP, lock temporarily after repeated failures, log suspicious activity, and require strong passwords plus multi-factor authentication.

OWASP recommendation

OWASP recommends secure authentication controls such as rate limiting, account lockout, strong password storage, monitoring, and multi-factor authentication.

Vulnerable vs secure implementation

The vulnerable version allows unlimited guessing. The secure version limits attempts and locks the account temporarily before the correct password can be reached.
